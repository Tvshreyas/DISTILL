| Pillar | % | Description |
|---|---|---|

| Building in public | 25% | Real numbers, lessons, decisions, founder perspective |
| Content repurposing | 20% | Blog post takeaways, glossary concepts, reflection prompts |

---

## Brand Voice on Social

- Lowercase. No exclamation marks. Periods at the end of thoughts.
- Short sentences. Line breaks between ideas.
- Never: praise, medical claims, hustle culture, "10x", "game-changer", "unlock your potential"
- Always: direct, factual, identity-framing ("a thinking tool"), slightly contrarian
- Tone reference: @naval meets @visakanv — compressed insight, no hype

## Week 2-4 Content Templates

### Thinking Provocations (use 3x/week)

> how many books have you read this year?
> now: how many original thoughts did you form about any of them?
> the gap between those two numbers is the problem.

> unpopular opinion: reading more books isn't the goal.
> thinking about fewer books is.

> your notes app has 2,000 entries.
> how many of them are YOUR thoughts vs. someone else's ideas you copied?

> the last podcast you listened to — can you articulate one thing you disagreed with?
> if not, you consumed it. you didn't process it.

> AI can summarize a book in 30 seconds.
> it still can't tell you what you think about it.
> that's the part that matters.

> "i read 50 books this year" is not an accomplishment.
> "i changed my mind about 3 things this year because of what i read" is.

> speed reading is optimizing for the wrong metric.
> comprehension without reflection is just faster forgetting.

> you don't have an information problem. you have a processing problem.
> the bottleneck isn't input. it's what you do after.

### Building in Public (use 2x/week)

> [genuine lesson — technical, product, or personal].

> honest update: [metric] is [good/bad].
> here's what i'm doing about it: [action].
> building in public means sharing the real numbers, not just the good ones.

> feature request i keep getting: [request].

## LinkedIn Content (1-2x/week)

LinkedIn posts should be longer (150-300 words), more professional, but same voice.

### Template 1: Problem Statement
> I read 40+ books last year.
>
> When someone asked me what I thought about any of them — not what the author said, but what I thought — I had nothing.
>
> This is the reading paradox: the more you consume, the less you process.
>
> [3-4 sentences expanding on the insight]
>
> I built Distill to solve this for myself. It's a simple tool: after reading/watching/listening to something, you write a short reflection. Your reflections resurface later.
>
> No AI. No summaries. Just: what did you think?
>
> distill.app

### Template 2: Data/Insight
> [Data point about reading/retention/attention]
>
> [What this means in practical terms]
>
> [How reflection addresses this]
>
> [Soft CTA to blog post or product]

### Template 3: Contrarian Take
> Unpopular opinion in [knowledge work / productivity / reading]:
>
> [Bold statement]
>
> Here's why: [3-4 sentences of reasoning]
>
> [Connection to thinking/reflection, no hard sell]

## Engagement Rules

### Daily (15 min)
- Reply to every comment on your posts
- Reply to every mention/RT
- Comment on 5 posts from accounts in the reading/productivity/building space

### Quality Comments (not "great post!")
- Add a related observation from your experience
- Ask a specific follow-up question
- Respectfully push back with nuance

### Accounts to Engage With
- Founders building in public (IndieHackers community on X)
- Book reviewers and "what I read this month" posters
- Anti-productivity and slow living accounts
- Anyone posting about attention, deep work, or reading retention

## What NOT to Post

- Screenshots of analytics dashboards (vanity metrics without context)
- "We just hit X users!" without reflection on what it means
- Anything that sounds like an ad: "Try Distill today!", "Sign up now!", "Don't miss out!"
- Emojis in every post (occasional is fine, not a pattern)
- Threads longer than 6 tweets (diminishing engagement after tweet 4)
- Hot takes you don't actually believe (contrarian for clicks)
- Anything that violates copy rules: no praise, no medical claims, no therapeutic framing
