## 4. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Opportunities

### Queries Most Likely to Trigger AI Answers

AI Overviews (Google), ChatGPT, and Perplexity generate answers for:

| Query Type             | Example                         | AI Answer Format             | How Distill Gets Cited                                                                            |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "How to" procedural    | "how to remember what you read" | Numbered step list           | Publish a definitive numbered-list post with a unique framework name (e.g., "The Distill Method") |
| "What is" definitional | "what is reflective reading"    | Short definition + expansion | Be the clearest, most concise definition source                                                   |
| "Best tools for X"     | "best app for book reflection"  | Bulleted tool list           | Get listed on comparison sites + own a dedicated landing page                                     |
| "Why" causal           | "why do I forget what I read"   | Science explanation + tips   | Cite the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve with original data/framing                                   |
| "X vs Y" comparison    | "passive vs active reading"     | Table or comparison          | Publish a clear comparison post with structured data                                              |

### Content Format for GEO Citation

Based on current GEO best practices for 2026:

1. **Answer in the first 200 words.** AI engines pull from the opening. Put the answer before the explanation.
2. **Use named frameworks.** "The Distill Method" or "The 3-Step Reflection Loop" gives AI something specific to cite.
3. **Include original data or statistics.** Even from your own user base: "Users who wrote reflections within 10 minutes of finishing a chapter retained 2x more key ideas" (run this study post-launch).
4. **Structured content.** Use H2/H3 headers that match common questions. Use numbered lists, tables, and definition lists.
5. **Author authority signals.** Publish under a real name with an author bio page. Link to the author's LinkedIn, Twitter. AI engines check E-E-A-T signals.
6. **Cite credible sources.** Reference Ebbinghaus, spaced repetition research, Cal Newport. AI engines trust pages that cite peer-reviewed work.
7. **Keep content fresh.** Add "Last Updated: [date]" and update quarterly. Recency is weighted in AI Overviews.

### Highest GEO-Potential Keywords for Distill

1. "what to do after reading a book" -- Procedural, high GEO trigger rate
2. "how to reflect on what you read" -- Definitional + procedural
3. "why do I forget everything I read" -- Causal, science-backed answer opportunity
4. "how to remember what you learn from podcasts" -- Underserved in AI answers
5. "passive vs active content consumption" -- No authoritative source exists
6. "book reflection prompts" -- List-format, high GEO citation potential
7. "spaced repetition for reading" -- Science-backed, definitional
8. "intentional content consumption" -- Emerging concept, Distill can define it

## 8. Technical SEO Notes for Content

1. **URL structure:** Use `/blog/[slug]` with keyword-rich slugs (already in place).
2. **Title tags:** Primary keyword first, brand last. Example: "What to Do After Reading a Book: 7 Activities | Distill"
3. **Meta descriptions:** Include primary keyword + CTA language. Under 155 characters.
4. **Schema markup:** Add `Article`, `HowTo`, or `FAQPage` JSON-LD to each post. This increases AI citation probability.
5. **Internal links:** Every post links to at least 2 other posts in the same cluster and 1 post in an adjacent cluster.
6. **Image alt text:** Descriptive, keyword-inclusive. No stuffing.
7. **Last Updated date:** Visible on every post. Update quarterly.
8. **Author page:** Create `/about` or `/blog/author/[name]` with credentials and social links.

## 9. Competitive Gaps to Exploit

### What competitors are NOT doing

1. **Readwise** focuses on highlights and recall. They do not talk about original thinking or reflection. Distill owns "reflection."
2. **Notion** is a blank canvas. No one searches "Notion for book reflection." Distill can own the vertical.
3. **Roam Research** targets PKM power users. Normal readers feel excluded. Distill is for everyone who reads.
4. **Goodreads/StoryGraph** are trackers, not thinking tools. They track what you read, not what you thought.
5. **No one** owns "podcast reflection" as a concept. Distill can define it.
6. **No one** owns "intentional content consumption" as a term. Distill can be the source AI engines cite.

### Content types competitors neglect

- Post-consumption reflection guides (not pre-reading or during-reading)
- Podcast and video retention content (everyone writes about books only)
- Frameworks with names (The Distill Method, The Reflection Loop)
- Comparison content from the "reflection" angle (not "note-taking" angle)
