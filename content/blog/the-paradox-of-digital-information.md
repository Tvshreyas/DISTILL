We are drowning in information but starving for wisdom. In 2026, the volume of data produced daily is astronomical, yet the average person's ability to recall meaningful insights from yesterday's consumption is at an all-time low.

### The Infinite Scroll vs. The Deep Dive

Social media platforms are designed for the scroll, not the stay. They reward speed and breadth over depth and nuance. This results in "fragmented knowledge" — a collection of headlines without a cohesive mental model. If this pattern sounds familiar, you might recognize it as [doomscrolling by another name](/blog/how-to-stop-doomscrolling).

> **Distill Perspective**: Shallow consumption is a form of cognitive junk food. It feels satisfying in the moment but leaves your mind malnourished. Distillation is the antidote.

### Building a Filter

To survive the information age, you must move from being a consumer to being a curator.

1. **Aggressive Unsubscribing**: If it doesn't add value, it shouldn't be in your feed.
2. **Active Reflection**: For every hour of consumption, give yourself ten minutes of synthesis.
3. **External Brain**: Use tools like Distill to capture the 1% of content that actually matters to your growth.

Knowledge isn't what you read; it's [what you remember](/blog/how-to-remember-what-you-read) and integrate. If you want a structured approach to fixing this, start with an [information diet](/blog/information-diet).
