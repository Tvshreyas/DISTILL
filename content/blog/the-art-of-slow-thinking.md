In a world of infinite scrolls and 15-second clips, our ability to think deeply is under attack. We consume more information than any generation in history, yet [we remember less of it](/blog/why-you-forget-everything-you-read).

### The Consumption Paradox

The more we consume, the less we actually _distill_. We act as pipes for information, letting it flow through us without ever staying to become part of our knowledge base.

### Moving Towards Reflection

[Reflection](/glossary/reflective-thinking) is the process of turning information into insight. It requires:

1. **Space**: Time away from the screen.
2. **Effort**: The active labor of writing your thoughts down.
3. **Compound Interest**: Revisiting your thoughts over time.

Distill was built to solve exactly this. By providing a neo-brutalist space for your reflections, we help you build the habit of thinking, not just consuming. If you want practical techniques, start with [how to remember what you read](/blog/how-to-remember-what-you-read). Or explore why [writing after reading](/blog/writing-to-think) is the simplest way to turn information into understanding.
